🏥 Hospital Management Analytics Dashboard

🚀 Project Overview

This project delivers an end-to-end analytics solution for hospital operations, focusing on revenue generation, operational efficiency, and financial risk.

Rather than just visualizing data, this dashboard identifies systemic inefficiencies and hidden risks in hospital performance using a structured data model and interactive reporting.

📊 Business Problem

Hospitals often track metrics like admissions and revenue but fail to answer:

1. Is growth actually improving performance?
2. Are operations becoming more efficient?
3. Where is revenue being lost?

This project addresses those gaps by analyzing admissions, treatments, billing, and payments together.

📁 Dataset & Data Model

Structured relational dataset including: Patients, Admissions, Treatments, Billing, Doctors, Departments, Calendar

🧩 Data Model Design

The project uses a relational data model built in Excel Power Pivot:

Patient → Admission → Treatment → Billing
Doctor → Department
Calendar → Admission Date

👉 This structure enables:

1. Time-based analysis
2. Department-level performance
3. End-to-end revenue tracking

🛠️ Tools & Techniques

1. Microsoft Excel
2. Power Pivot (Data Modeling)
3. Pivot Tables
4. KPI Design
5. Data Cleaning & Transformation

📊 Dashboard Features

1. 📈 Admissions & Revenue Trends (monthly analysis)
2. 🏥 Department Performance (admission contribution)
3. 👨‍⚕️ Top Doctors (revenue contribution)
4. 💳 Payment Status Analysis
5. 🛏️ Treatment Type Distribution
6. 📅 Year-based slicer for dynamic filtering

📌 KPI Panel:
a. Total Admissions
b. Total Revenue
c. Pending Payment %
d. Avg Length of Stay
e. Avg Treatment Cost

🔍 Key Insights (Core Findings)

1. Growth Without Improvement
Admissions and revenue show steady linear growth
No acceleration or structural improvement

👉 Growth is driven by volume, not strategy

2. Operational Efficiency is Stagnant
Average Length of Stay ≈ 7.4–7.5 days consistently

👉 No improvement in: Patient throughput, Bed utilization, Process efficiency

3. Revenue Concentration Risk
Majority revenue comes from:
Oncology
Pediatrics
Cardiology
Several departments contribute minimally

👉 Over-dependence on a few departments = fragile revenue model

4. Payment Risk is Significant
~25% revenue remains pending

👉 This creates: Cash flow instability, Collection risk, Overstated financial performance

5. Weak Payment Strategy
Payments dominated by Cash/Card
Low EMI adoption

👉 Indicates: Limited financial flexibility for patients, Missed opportunity to improve conversion

6. Cost Structure is Static
Treatment costs show slight inflation only
No major pricing optimization

👉 Pricing strategy is passive, not strategic

7. Revenue Driven by Room Volume
General & Private rooms generate most revenue

👉 Growth comes from: Higher admissions NOT Higher value per patient

8. Core Conclusion

This hospital is growing — but inefficiently.

No operational improvement
No pricing strategy
No payment optimization

👉 Growth is volume-driven, not performance-driven

💡 Business Recommendations (THIS is what most people miss)
1. Improve payment collection systems → reduce pending %
2. Introduce EMI / flexible payment plans → increase conversion
3. Optimize hospital stay duration → improve efficiency
4. Diversify department revenue → reduce dependency risk
5. Implement pricing strategy → increase AOV per patient
